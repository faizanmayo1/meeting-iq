Opening · 60 seconds

02

What you say first

Stefan, before I open the product — one question. On a normal Monday, by the time you sit down with coffee, how many meetings has your leadership already had? Five? Ten? And of those, how many produced a decision someone owns by Friday? MeetingIQ's job is to make every one of those meetings produce an outcome — automatically.

Why this opening works

It anchors the demo to a real moment in their week and contrasts volume of meetings with quality of outcomes — the gap every operations leader feels but can't measure. Don't open with capabilities. Open with the gap.

Set the rules of the room

  • This is live. Data is seeded with Northwind's Q3 enterprise rollout — Halcyon Health, Mercer & Pratt, your real lanes of conflict.
  • Promise: three concrete dollar moments in the next 22 minutes. They don't need to keep notes — you'll send the recap.
  • Invite interrupts. The best demos have the buyer reaching for the trackpad.

If asked "what is this exactly?"

Think of MeetingIQ as a layer above Zoom, Slack, and Jira. It reads every meeting transcript, extracts decisions and actions with owners and deadlines, syncs them to your task systems, and tells you when two teams just made conflicting decisions. Your tools stay where they are. Decision-making moves up.

Objection handling

11

The five things Stefan and his peers ask in every demo. Memorize the bolded answer.

If they say…You say…
"How is this different from Otter.ai or Fellow?" Those tools answer what was said. MeetingIQ answers what was decided, who owns it, what it depends on, and is it conflicting with another team's decision. We're an orchestration layer on top of the meeting transcript — that's a different product category.
"Will leadership trust the AI to extract decisions?" Default everything to Human Approval. Owners get a Slack notification with the AI-extracted action and click Approve or Edit. After 30 days they shift the toggles to Auto themselves — once they see it's right 9 times out of 10, the platform earns its way in.
"What's the ROI?" One avoided cross-team conflict pays for the year. Across our reference customers: 5.8 hours / week reclaimed per IC, 41% faster decision velocity, 18-point lift in meeting effectiveness. Average payback: 3 months.
"How long is implementation?" One week to read-only. Three weeks to first AI-extracted action. Eight weeks to full agent workflows on. We start with one team's recurring meetings — once the leaders see it work, they pull us into everything.
"What about privacy — we have sensitive leadership meetings." Sensitive meetings can be classified at the calendar level. Transcripts are encrypted at rest, retrieval is permission-aware so only authorized members can ask the Knowledge layer about them, and every read is audit logged. You stay in control of who sees what.
